Water Safety Tips

Learn about water purification, diseases, and safe practices

üíß Water Purification Methods

üî• Boiling

The simplest and most effective method. Boil water for at least 1 minute (3 minutes at high altitudes) to kill most pathogens and microorganisms.

üî¨ Filtration

Using water filters removes particles, sediment, and some microorganisms. Use ceramic, sand, or activated carbon filters for better results.

Place water in a clear bottle in direct sunlight for 6-8 hours. UV rays and heat kill harmful bacteria and viruses.

üß™ Chemical Treatment

Chlorine tablets, bleach, or potassium permanganate can disinfect water. Follow instructions carefully for proper dosage.

‚ö° Advanced Methods

Reverse osmosis, UV sterilization, and ozonation are modern purification techniques used in water treatment plants.

ü¶† Diseases from Unsafe Water

Cholera

Caused by bacteria, leads to severe dehydration and diarrhea

Typhoid

Bacterial infection causing fever and abdominal pain

Dysentery

Intestinal infection causing bloody diarrhea and cramps

Hepatitis A

Viral infection affecting the liver, causes jaundice

Giardiasis

Parasitic infection causing persistent diarrhea

Cryptosporidiosis

Parasitic disease affecting the intestines

üè∫ Safe Water Storage Tips

‚úÖ Use Clean Containers

Store water in food-grade containers that are clean and have tight-fitting lids to prevent contamination.

‚úÖ Keep in Cool, Dark Place

Store water away from sunlight and heat sources to prevent algae growth and microbial multiplication.

‚úÖ Replace Regularly

Change stored water every 6 months to prevent bacterial growth and maintain freshness.

‚úÖ Label Your Water

Mark the date of storage and the purification method used for easy reference.

‚úÖ Proper Handling

Always use clean utensils or taps to draw water. Avoid direct contact with hands to prevent contamination.
